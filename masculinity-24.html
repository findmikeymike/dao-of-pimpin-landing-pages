Raul and Gregory grabbed the brown manila envelopes tagged with their names, folded them down the center, shoved them in their pockets and headed out the door a moment behind The Master. As they made their way out the front door of the building, Raul scanned the streets, expecting to get one last glimpse of the Master walking off into the distance, but the Master was nowhere in sight. As if by real Magic, the man had disappeared back into the mystical ether from which he first emerged. As Raul pondered the mystery of the Master and surveyed the unknown world around him, he heard Gregory’s voice. “My brother… I guess there was room for two to walk the Path after all.” Raul looked back at Gregory with a warm smile. “Yes… I suppose the Master’s tests and games knew no limit,” Raul responded. “There’s a tavern at the end of this block, shall we start our journey with a drink?” said Gregory. “I don’t think that’s a bad idea, my friend."
